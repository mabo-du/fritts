I am building an open-source desktop tool for tree-ring cross-dating, measurement, and master chronology building. Existing software is often dated, proprietary, or lacks a modern visual interface.

RESEARCH PROMPT 1:

**Tree-Ring Data Formats**: What are the standard formats for dendrochronology data (e.g., Tucson decadal format, Heidelberg format, TRiDaS)? How does the TRiDaS (Tree Ring Data Standard) schema structure metadata for archaeological timber?

RESEARCH PROMPT 2:

**Cross-dating Algorithms**: What statistical algorithms are used to cross-match tree-ring width sequences against master chronologies? Explain the mathematics behind the t-value (Baillie-Pilcher and Hollstein variants) and Gleichläufigkeit (Coefficient of Parallel Variation).

RESEARCH PROMPT 3:

**Image Processing for Ring Widths**: What computer vision techniques can be used to automatically or semi-automatically detect and measure tree-ring boundaries from high-resolution scans of wood cores or slices?

RESEARCH PROMPT 4:

**Existing Dendrochronology Software**: What software is currently used in dendrochronology labs (e.g., TSAP-Win, CooRecorder, CDendro, dplR)? What are the primary pain points for archaeologists using these tools?

RESEARCH PROMPT 5:

**Master Chronology Databases**: Where are master regional tree-ring chronologies hosted (e.g., ITRDB)? Does the International Tree-Ring Data Bank provide APIs for programmatic access and querying?


RESEARCH PROMPT 6:

**UI for Visual Cross-matching**: What are the best UX patterns for visually aligning and editing extremely long time-series data (tree-ring curves)? How can a UI handle zooming, panning, and adding/removing phantom rings interactively?
